CRMicro SiC Technology: Advantages and Applications

Understanding silicon carbide benefits and optimal applications

CRMicro SiC Technology: Advantages and Applications

By Jane Smith, Senior Application Engineer

January 15, 2025

Reading time: 10 min

Silicon Carbide (SiC) technology represents a significant advancement in power semiconductor design, offering superior performance compared to traditional silicon devices in many applications. CRMicro's silicon carbide portfolio includes both SiC diodes and SiC MOSFETs, engineered to maximize the benefits of wide bandgap technology.

What Makes SiC Superior to Silicon?

Silicon Carbide has an inherently wider bandgap (3.3 eV) compared to silicon (1.1 eV), which results in several key advantages:

Higher Breakdown Field Strength

SiC has approximately 10 times higher breakdown field strength than silicon, enabling devices with much higher voltage ratings in a smaller die size. This translates to lower on-resistance per unit area (RDS(on) × A), especially beneficial for high-voltage devices (650V and above).

Higher Thermal Conductivity

SiC has three times better thermal conductivity than silicon, allowing for better heat dissipation and higher power density designs. CRMicro SiC devices can operate at junction temperatures up to 200°C, compared to 150°C for silicon devices.

Higher Saturation Electron Velocity

This enables faster switching speeds with lower switching losses, making SiC ideal for high-frequency applications.

CRMicro SiC Device Performance

SiC Diodes (Schottky Barrier Diodes)

CRMicro SiC diodes offer several advantages over traditional silicon diodes:

  • Zero recovery charge - eliminates reverse recovery losses
  • Temperature stable behavior - forward voltage drops slightly with temperature
  • High efficiency - especially in PFC circuits and high-frequency power supplies

Typical applications include PFC circuits, power supplies, and welders where efficiency and thermal performance are critical.

SiC MOSFETs

CRMicro SiC MOSFETs combine the benefits of SiC material properties with advanced device design:

  • Low RDS(on) - lower conduction losses than silicon devices
  • Low switching losses - enables higher frequency operation
  • Temperature stability - RDS(on)
  • Body diode characteristics - improved performance in hard-switching applications

Optimal Applications for CRMicro SiC Devices

Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure

SiC devices excel in EV charging applications due to their ability to operate at higher frequencies and temperatures. The lower switching and conduction losses enable more compact, efficient charging systems. CRMicro SiC MOSFETs and diodes are ideal for both on-board chargers (OBC) and charging station power supplies.

Solar Inverters

SiC's superior performance enhances solar inverter efficiency, particularly in string inverters where higher switching frequencies are desired. The improved thermal performance allows for smaller heatsinks and better power density.

Data Center Power Supplies

For PFC stages and DC-DC converters in data centers, SiC devices enable higher efficiency and power density. The higher switching frequency capability allows for smaller magnetic components, reducing system size and cost.

Energy Storage Systems

SiC's temperature stability and low losses benefit bi-directional DC-DC converters in energy storage applications, where thermal performance and efficiency across varying load conditions are critical.

Design Considerations for SiC Implementation

Gate Drive Requirements

SiC MOSFETs have different gate drive requirements compared to silicon:

  • Positive gate voltage: Typically 18-20V for full enhancement
  • Gate resistance: Lower values may be needed for fast switching
  • Oscillation considerations: Critical to minimize parasitic inductance

Switching Characteristics

SiC devices can switch much faster than silicon, generating higher dV/dt and dI/dt. Consider:

  • Board layout to minimize parasitic inductance
  • Snubber circuits if needed to limit voltage overshoot
  • EMI considerations at higher switching frequencies

Thermal Design

While SiC can operate at higher temperatures, proper thermal design remains critical:

  • Thermal interface materials with high conductivity
  • Optimized heatsink design for high power density
  • Temperature monitoring for system protection

Economic Advantages of SiC

While SiC devices have higher upfront costs than silicon, they provide economic benefits:

  • System cost reduction through smaller magnetics, capacitors, and heatsinks
  • Higher efficiency reducing operational costs
  • Higher power density reducing system size and installation costs
  • Longer system life through better thermal performance

Design Tip

When implementing CRMicro SiC MOSFETs in high-frequency designs, consider using the zero reverse recovery charge of SiC diodes in synchronous rectifier configurations. This can significantly reduce switching losses compared to silicon implementations.

CRMicro SiC Portfolio

CRMicro offers SiC devices in various voltage and current ratings:

  • SiC Diodes: 650V, 1200V in various current ratings
  • SiC MOSFETs: 650V and 1200V in TO-247, D2PAK, and other packages
  • Hybrid modules: Combining SiC diodes with silicon IGBTs for optimal performance

Conclusion

CRMicro's silicon carbide technology offers significant advantages over traditional silicon devices, particularly in applications requiring high efficiency, high power density, and high-temperature operation. As SiC technology matures and costs continue to decrease, its adoption will expand to even more applications in power electronics.

When considering SiC for your design, evaluate the total system benefits rather than just component cost. The efficiency, size, and performance advantages of CRMicro SiC devices often justify the higher component cost through overall system value improvements.
