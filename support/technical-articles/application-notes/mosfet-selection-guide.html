How to Select the Right CRMicro MOSFET for Your Application

A comprehensive guide by our field application engineers on MOSFET selection

How to Select the Right CRMicro MOSFET for Your Application

By John Doe, Principal FAE | Power Electronics

January 15, 2025

Reading time: 8 min

Selecting the right MOSFET for your application is crucial for achieving optimal performance, efficiency, and reliability. With CRMicro's comprehensive portfolio of power MOSFETs, including SGT (Shielded Gate Trench), Super Junction (SJ), and Planar technologies, engineers have a range of options to suit different applications.

Key Parameters for MOSFET Selection

When selecting a MOSFET, several critical parameters must be evaluated:

Voltage Rating (VDS)

The drain-to-source voltage rating must exceed the maximum voltage in your application with an appropriate safety margin. As a rule of thumb, select a MOSFET with a voltage rating 20-50% higher than the maximum expected voltage. CRMicro offers MOSFETs from 20V to 1500V.

Current Rating (ID)

The continuous drain current rating must handle your application's peak current requirements. Consider both continuous and pulsed current ratings, as well as the operating temperature derating. CRMicro's MOSFETs offer current ratings from under 1A to over 200A.

On-Resistance (RDS(on))

This is crucial for conduction losses. Lower RDS(on) reduces power dissipation but typically increases capacitance and cost. Balance RDS(on) with switching losses for your frequency.

Thermal Considerations

Calculate the junction temperature to ensure it remains within safe limits. Consider thermal resistance (RθJA) and the application's cooling capabilities.

CRMicro MOSFET Technologies

CRMicro offers several MOSFET technologies optimized for different applications:

SGT (Shielded Gate Trench) MOSFETs

CRMicro's SGT technology provides the industry's lowest RDS(on) × Qg figure-of-merit, making them ideal for low-voltage, high-efficiency applications like point-of-load converters, battery management, and power tools. These devices excel in applications where minimizing conduction and switching losses is critical.

Super Junction (SJ) MOSFETs

CRMicro's SJ MOSFETs offer an optimal balance of low RDS(on) and fast switching performance, making them perfect for PFC circuits, adapters, and high-power industrial applications. They provide superior performance in the 600V to 800V range compared to planar technology.

Planar MOSFETs

For applications requiring high reliability and stable characteristics, CRMicro's planar MOSFETs offer robust performance across temperature and operating conditions. These are ideal for automotive and industrial applications where long-term stability is critical.

Application-Specific Considerations

Different applications have unique requirements:

Power Supplies

For switch-mode power supplies, prioritize RDS(on) for low-voltage applications and the RDS(on) × Qg trade-off for high-frequency designs. CRMicro SGT MOSFETs are excellent for POL converters, while SJ MOSFETs excel in PFC stages.

Motor Drives

Motor drive applications need devices that can handle high current pulses and have good body diode characteristics. Consider Crss (feedback capacitance) for di/dt control during switching.

Battery Management

Battery applications require low RDS(on) to minimize losses and good thermal performance. CRMicro's low-voltage SGT MOSFETs offer excellent performance for battery pack switches and protection circuits.

Thermal Design Considerations

Proper thermal design is critical to maximize MOSFET performance and reliability:

Calculate total power dissipation including conduction and switching losses. Use the formula:

PTOTAL = PCONDUCTION + PSWITCHING
PCONDUCTION = IRMS2 × RDS(on)
PSWITCHING = (EON + EOFF) × fSW

Ensure the junction temperature remains below the maximum rating (typically 150°C or 175°C). Use the thermal equation:

TJ = TA + PD × RθJA

Package Selection

Package choice affects thermal performance and PCB area. CRMicro offers a wide range of packages:

  • TO-220, TO-247 for through-hole applications
  • DPAK, D2PAK for mid-power surface mount applications
  • SO-8, PowerPAK for space-constrained high-frequency applications
  • LFPAK for high-current applications

Design Checklist

Before finalizing your MOSFET selection, verify:

  • Maximum voltage has sufficient margin
  • Current rating handles both continuous and peak currents
  • Thermal design keeps junction temperature within limits
  • Package is appropriate for power dissipation and space constraints
  • Driver compatibility for gate charge requirements
  • Avalanche energy rating for inductive loads

Expert Tip from our FAEs

When designing with CRMicro MOSFETs, consider using our dual MOSFET solutions for synchronous rectification. These devices integrate control and synchronous rectifier MOSFETs in a single package, reducing component count and improving efficiency in flyback and forward converters.

Conclusion

Selecting the right CRMicro MOSFET involves balancing multiple parameters based on your specific application. Consider voltage, current, thermal, and switching requirements alongside cost and availability. When in doubt, consult with our field application engineers for assistance with your design.
