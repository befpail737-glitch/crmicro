CRMicro Announces New SiC Technology for Next-Generation Power Electronics

Industry-leading silicon carbide technology enhances power conversion efficiency

January 15, 2025

By CRMicro News Team

CRMicro today announced the release of its latest generation silicon carbide (SiC) technology, designed to advance power electronics performance across multiple industries. The new SiC MOSFETs and diodes offer significant improvements in efficiency, power density, and thermal performance compared to traditional silicon solutions.

Enhanced Performance Features

The new SiC technology incorporates several breakthrough features that set new benchmarks for power semiconductor performance:

  • Ultra-low on-resistance: Achieving industry-leading RDS(on) values with improved cell design
  • Superior thermal conductivity: SiC's inherent 3x better thermal conductivity enables higher power density designs
  • Zero recovery charge: Eliminates reverse recovery losses in switching applications
  • High temperature operation: Stable performance up to 200°C junction temperature
  • High switching frequency capability: Enables smaller magnetics and increased power density

Target Applications

The new SiC technology is optimized for applications requiring high efficiency and power density:

Electric Vehicle Charging

SiC devices enable higher power density in EV charging infrastructure, reducing the size and weight of charging stations while improving efficiency. The technology is particularly beneficial for DC fast chargers operating at 300kW and beyond.

Photovoltaic Systems

Solar inverters utilizing SiC technology achieve higher efficiency, especially in string inverters where higher switching frequencies reduce magnetics size without sacrificing performance. The improved thermal characteristics also enhance reliability in outdoor installations.

Data Center Power Supplies

SiC components reduce both conduction and switching losses in PFC stages and isolated DC-DC converters, contributing to overall data center energy efficiency improvements.

Industrial Power Systems

The superior thermal performance and high temperature operation capability make SiC ideal for harsh industrial environments and high-power applications.

Technical Improvements

Compared to previous generation SiC devices, the new technology offers:

Parameter Previous Gen New Gen Improvement
RDS(on) (normalized) 100% 85% 15% lower
Qg (gate charge) 100% 90% 10% lower
Qrr (reverse recovery charge) 100% 95% 5% lower
Thermal resistance 100% 90% 10% lower

Availability and Packaging

The new SiC technology is available in multiple packaging options to suit different application requirements:

  • Discrete packages: TO-247-4L, TO-220, D2PAK, and other standard packages
  • Surface-mount options: PowerPAK, LFPAK, and other small-footprint packages
  • Industrial modules: Various module configurations for high-power applications

Production volumes are available now, with qualified foundry partners ensuring stable supply for high-volume applications.

Environmental Impact

The higher efficiency offered by SiC technology contributes significantly to energy conservation. In data center applications alone, SiC-based power supplies can reduce total energy consumption by 0.5-1%, translating to substantial carbon emission reductions at scale.

"Our new SiC technology represents a significant leap forward in power semiconductor performance," said Dr. Chen Wei, VP of Technology at CRMicro. "These devices will enable the next generation of efficient power systems across multiple industries."

Testing and Qualification

The new SiC technology has undergone rigorous qualification testing:

  • Highly Accelerated Stress Testing (HAST) for moisture resistance
  • Temperature Cycling (TC) for thermal stability
  • High Temperature Gate Bias (HTGB) for gate oxide reliability
  • Avalanche Energy (EAS) testing for robustness
  • Long-term stability testing under various operating conditions

Market Outlook

Industry analysts project the SiC power semiconductor market to grow at over 25% CAGR through 2028, driven by electric vehicle adoption and renewable energy initiatives. CRMicro's new technology positions the company to capture a greater share of this expanding market.

Initial customer feedback has been overwhelmingly positive, with several design wins already secured in high-power applications requiring >99% efficiency.

Design Support Available

CRMicro's field application engineering team is available to support customers transitioning to the new SiC technology. Evaluation samples and reference designs are available through authorized distributors.

Conclusion

CRMicro's new SiC technology represents a significant advancement in power semiconductor capabilities, offering improved performance across multiple key parameters. Combined with competitive pricing and stable supply, the technology is positioned to accelerate SiC adoption across various market segments.
